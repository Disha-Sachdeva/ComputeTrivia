Did You Know ?

The term "bug" in computing originated when a moth got trapped in a relay of the Harvard Mark II computer in 1947, leading to the first recorded instance of debugging.

Did You Know ?

The concept of a computer "mouse" was inspired by its physical resemblance to the rodent. Douglas Engelbart, the inventor of the computer mouse, chose the name because of the device's tail-like cable and its ability to "skitter" across the desktop.

Did You Know ?

The Konami Code, a cheat code sequence (up, up, down, down, left, right, left, right, B, A) invented in 1986 by Kazuhisa Hashimoto, has become iconic in video games and is often used as an Easter egg in software and websites.

Did You Know ?

The world's first computer virus, named "Creeper," was created in the early 1970s. It spread through ARPANET, the precursor to the internet, and displayed the message "I'm the creeper, catch me if you can!"

Did You Know ?

The world's first website, created by Tim Berners-Lee in 1991, was dedicated to providing information about the World Wide Web project. It explained how to create web pages and included links to other relevant resources.

Did You Know ?

The phrase "cookies" in the context of computer science originated from the term "magic cookie", used in UNIX systems to refer to data packets exchanged between programs. In web browsers, cookies store user data to enhance browsing experiences.
